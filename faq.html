Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

BMI stands for body mass index and attempts to estimate one’s body fat in relation to one’s height. A person’s weight(kg) divided by their height(m) is the body mass index. A normal BMI should be in the range 18.5-24.9. Under or over is considered underweight or overweight respectively.

In order to lose weight, you need to be in a calorie deficit. This means you need to be burning more calories than you are consuming. You can do this by lowering your caloric intake or increasing your physical activity.

According to the CDC, adults should get at least 150 minutes of moderate intensity exercise every week.

Resting heart rates can vary drastically, although typically adults will have a heart rate between 60-100 beats per minute. An individual with a healthy cardiovascular system will typically have a lower resting heart rate than the average individual.

Sleep is one of the most important elements to properly recovering from your workout! The CDC recommends getting at least 7 hours of sleep for an adult.
